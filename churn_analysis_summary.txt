
================================================================================
                    CHURN ANALYSIS - EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
                         STREAMING SERVICE DATASET
================================================================================

DATASET OVERVIEW:
• Total Customers: 500
• Churned Customers: 193 (38.6%)
• Retained Customers: 307 (61.4%)

================================================================================
                         MAJOR DRIVERS OF CHURN
================================================================================

1. CUSTOMER SERVICE INTERACTIONS (STRONGEST PREDICTOR)
   ⚠️ Effect Size: 0.592 (Large effect)
   ⚠️ Statistical Significance: p < 0.0001
   
   KEY FINDING: Churned customers contact customer service 28% MORE often
   • Churned customers average: 3.18 interactions/year
   • Retained customers average: 2.49 interactions/year
   • Difference: +0.69 interactions
   
   INTERPRETATION: High customer service interaction is a LEADING INDICATOR
   of dissatisfaction and imminent churn. Customers who frequently contact
   support are likely experiencing persistent issues.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2. VIEWING ENGAGEMENT (PROTECTIVE FACTOR)
   ✓ Effect Size: -0.523 (Large effect)
   ✓ Statistical Significance: p < 0.0001
   
   KEY FINDING: Churned customers watch 20% LESS content
   • Churned customers average: 66.6 hours/month
   • Retained customers average: 83.2 hours/month
   • Difference: -16.6 hours/month
   
   INTERPRETATION: Low engagement with content is a strong predictor of churn.
   Customers who don't watch much content see less value in the subscription.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3. BINGE-WATCHING BEHAVIOR (PROTECTIVE FACTOR)
   ✓ Effect Size: -0.503 (Large effect)
   ✓ Statistical Significance: p < 0.0001
   
   KEY FINDING: Churned customers have 20% FEWER binge-watching sessions
   • Churned customers average: 6.17 sessions/month
   • Retained customers average: 7.69 sessions/month
   • Difference: -1.52 sessions
   
   INTERPRETATION: Binge-watching indicates content engagement and addiction.
   Customers who binge-watch are more invested in the platform.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4. CONTENT DIVERSITY (PROTECTIVE FACTOR)
   ✓ Effect Size: -0.472 (Medium-to-Large effect)
   ✓ Statistical Significance: p < 0.0001
   
   KEY FINDING: Churned customers explore 18% FEWER unique titles
   • Churned customers average: 19.5 unique titles/month
   • Retained customers average: 23.7 unique titles/month
   • Difference: -4.3 titles
   
   INTERPRETATION: Customers who explore diverse content find more value.
   Limited exploration suggests they're not finding content they enjoy.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5. SESSION DURATION (PROTECTIVE FACTOR)
   ✓ Effect Size: -0.460 (Medium effect)
   ✓ Statistical Significance: p < 0.0001
   
   KEY FINDING: Churned customers have 14% SHORTER viewing sessions
   • Churned customers average: 49.4 minutes/session
   • Retained customers average: 57.8 minutes/session
   • Difference: -8.3 minutes
   
   INTERPRETATION: Shorter sessions may indicate inability to find engaging
   content or frequent interruptions in viewing experience.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

6. SUBSCRIPTION TIER (CATEGORICAL DRIVER)
   ⚠️ Effect Size: Cramér's V = 0.138
   ⚠️ Statistical Significance: p = 0.0086
   
   KEY FINDING: Churn rate varies significantly by tier
   • Basic Tier: 43.5% churn rate (HIGHEST RISK)
   • Standard Tier: 39.5% churn rate
   • Premium Tier: 24.1% churn rate (LOWEST RISK)
   
   INTERPRETATION: Basic tier customers are 80% more likely to churn than
   Premium customers. This may be due to value perception or feature limitations.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

7. CONTENT PREFERENCE (GENRE)
   Note: Not statistically significant (p = 0.118), but shows patterns
   
   Highest Risk Genres:
   • Horror: 52.3% churn rate
   • Thriller: 48.3% churn rate
   • Action: 44.6% churn rate
   
   Lowest Risk Genres:
   • Documentary: 25.9% churn rate
   • Comedy: 33.0% churn rate
   • Drama: 35.3% churn rate

================================================================================
                         ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

🎯 IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:

1. PROACTIVE CUSTOMER SERVICE INTERVENTION
   • Implement early warning system for customers with 3+ CS interactions
   • Conduct satisfaction surveys after each interaction
   • Assign dedicated account managers for high-interaction customers
   • Root cause analysis: Fix underlying issues causing multiple contacts

2. RE-ENGAGEMENT CAMPAIGNS FOR LOW VIEWERS
   • Alert system for customers watching < 70 hours/month
   • Personalized content recommendations to increase viewing
   • "Discover new shows" email campaigns
   • Limited-time content highlights to drive engagement

3. CONTENT DISCOVERY IMPROVEMENTS
   • Enhance recommendation algorithm for variety
   • "Something different" feature to encourage exploration
   • Personalized genre mixing to broaden viewing
   • Reduce friction in content discovery

4. BINGE-WORTHY CONTENT PROMOTION
   • Highlight complete series for binge-watching
   • Auto-play next episode improvements
   • "Perfect for binge-watching" content tags
   • Weekend binge-watch suggestions

5. BASIC TIER OPTIMIZATION
   • Assess feature gaps causing high Basic tier churn
   • A/B test upgrade incentives for Basic customers
   • Consider adding features to Basic tier to improve retention
   • Personalized upgrade offers showing clear value proposition

6. SESSION QUALITY IMPROVEMENTS
   • Investigate technical issues causing short sessions
   • Improve streaming quality and reduce buffering
   • Better pause/resume functionality
   • Optimize mobile viewing experience

================================================================================
                              KEY TAKEAWAYS
================================================================================

1. Customer service interactions are the #1 red flag for churn
   → Focus on issue resolution, not just interaction management

2. Engagement metrics (viewing hours, binge sessions) are protective
   → Drive engagement through better content and recommendations

3. Content exploration diversity is crucial for retention
   → Improve discovery features and recommendation variety

4. Basic tier customers need attention - 80% higher churn than Premium
   → Evaluate tier value proposition and feature gaps

5. All top drivers are actionable - no demographic factors dominate
   → Company can directly influence churn through operational improvements
